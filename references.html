Sweden’s Datasets

The Change of Sweden’s Forest Area Over Time in Million Hectares (2000-2020)

The data table, Arable land and forest land by region and land use category. Year 1951 – 2020, from Statistics Sweden (2022) counts forest land as:

Land spanning more than 0.5 hectares with trees higher than 5 meters and a canopy cover of more than 10 percent, or trees able to reach these thresholds in situ. Total forest land is the sum of productive forest and non-productive forest. (Statistics Sweden, 2022, total forest land)
This data was used to create the chart for Sweden’s forest area change over time.

The variables used from Statistics Sweden (2022) for Sweden’s forest area change over time chart are the following:
  • region: 00 Sweden
  • land use category: total forest land
  • Year: 2020, 2015, 2010, 2005, 2000

The total forest area variable is used in the x-axis of the chart. The values in the variable were divided by 1 000 000, converting the unit from hectares to million hectares to make reading the values from the chart easier. The year variable was used in the y-axis of the chart to show the forest area change over time. The data is viewed in a form of an area chart.

Average Annual Felling Volume in Swedish Regions in Million Forest Cubic Meters (2000-2020)

The data tabel 4.1, Mean annual felling by tree species and region. All land use classes (1982 - date), from SLU Riksskogstaxeringen (2025) was used to create the chart for Sweden’s average annual felling in each region.

The year variable is “Five year average, represented by the middle year e.g. 2019/20-2023/24=2021. Year represents the start of a felling season e.g. 2023 represents the period from bud opening 2023 to bud opening 2024.” (SLU Riksskogstaxeringen, 2025, Year).

The variables used from SLU Riksskogstaxeringen (2025) for Sweden’s average annual felling in each region chart are the following:

  • Table contents: mill. m³sk
  • Year: 2020, 2019, 2018, 2017, 2016, 2015, 2014, 2013, 2012, 2011, 2010, 2009, 2008, 2007, 2006, 2005, 2004, 2003, 2002, 2001, 2000
  • Region: 101 N Norrland, 102 S Norrland, 103 Svealand, 104 Götaland
  • Tree species: All tree species

The values of felling for each year where averaged for each region. This is done by summing the values of all the years, for each region, and dividing the sum by the number of all years (21). The average was then used in the y-axis of the chart and regions in the x-axis. The data is viewed in a form of a bar chart.

Average Annual Forest Growth Volume in Swedish Regions in Million Forest Cubic Meters (2000-2020)

The data tabel 2.14, Mean annual volume increment by tree species. Forest land. Year represent the average year for the years of increment (2002 - date), from SLU Riksskogstaxeringen (2025) was used to create the chart for Sweden’s average annual forest volume increase in each region.

The data includes growth of felled trees. “Forest area according to the Swedish Forestry Act, see chapter 4 of Skogsdata (available as a pdf from www.slu.se/nfi). Forest land.” (SLU Riksskogstaxeringen, 2025, Obs).

“Year represent the average year for the years of increment, e.g. 2019 represent years of increment 2015-2023 (years of inventory 2020-2024).” (SLU Riksskogstaxeringen, 2025, Year (Average)).

The variables used from SLU Riksskogstaxeringen (2025) for Sweden’s average annual forest volume increase in each region chart are the following:

  • Table contents: Mean annual volume increment (10000 m³sk)
  • Year (average): 2018, 2017, 2016, 2015, 2014, 2013, 2012, 2011, 2010, 2009, 2008, 2007, 2006, 2005, 2004, 2003, 2002
  • County: 101 N Norrland, 102 S Norrland, 103 Svealand, 104 Götaland
  • Alpine area: Excl. Alpine areas
  • Tree species: All tree species

The values of volume increment for each year where averaged for each region. This is done by summing the values of all the years, for each region, and dividing the sum by the number of all years (17). It was also then divided by 100 to convert the unit to million forest cubic meters (m³sk). The average was then used in the y-axis of the chart and regions in the x-axis. The data is viewed in a form of a bar chart.
Percentage of Protected Forest Area in Swedish Regions in (2023)

The data table, Formally protected forest land, voluntary set-asides, consideration patches and unproductive forest land. Year 2018 – 2023,, from Statistics Sweden (2024) is used for Sweden’s map for percentages of protected forest area.

The percentage share is determined by:

The proportion in percent has been calculated using non-rounded values in hectares. In the calculation, the numerator consists of the areas of the selected variables. The denominator consists of the area of forest land according to the official statistics on forest land in Sweden in the Swedish National Forest Inventory with regard to the forms Voluntary set-asides, Consideration patches, and Unproductive forest land. In the calculation of the proportion of Formally protected forest land, the denominator consists of the area of forest land according to the Swedish data on land cover mapping (NMD). Since the NMD is the source for the area in the numerator, that is, formal protection area, the quality of the statistics are considered to be enhanced if the same source is used for both the numerator and the denominator. (Statistics Sweden, 2024, Percentage share)

The division of Sweden’s regions:
  • Northern Norrland
    • A division of Sweden into four regions, adapted to the county borders. Each region corresponds to the total area of a specific number of counties.
    Northern Norrland: The counties of Norrbotten and Västerbotten.
  • Southern Norrland
    • A division of Sweden into four regions, adapted to the county borders. Each region corresponds to the total area of a specific number of counties.
    Southern Norrland: The counties of Jämtland, Västernorrland, and Gävleborg.
  • Svealand
    • A division of Sweden into four regions, adapted to the county borders. Each region corresponds to the total area of a specific number of counties.
    Svealand: The counties of Dalarna, Värmland, Örebro, Västmanland, Uppsala, Stockholm, and Södermanland.
  • Götaland
    • A division of Sweden into four regions, adapted to the county borders. Each region corresponds to the total area of a specific number of counties.
    Götaland: The counties of Östergötland, Gotland, Västra Götaland, Jönköping, Kronoberg, Kalmar, Halland, Blekinge, and Skåne. (Statistics Sweden, 2024, region)

The variables used from Statistics Sweden (2024) for Sweden’s map for percentages of protected forest area are the following:
  • observations: Percentage share
  • region: Nothern Norrland, Southern Norrland, Svealand, Götaland
  • Overlapping forms: without overlapping areas
  • Forest land: Total of forest land
  • Forms: Formally protected forest land
  • year: 2023
The percentage share of forest area is set in a color scale on the map. The map of Sweden is divided into the four regions; Upper Norrland (Nothern Norrland), Lower Norrland (Southern Norrland), Svealand and Götaland. The data is shown on a map of Sweden.
