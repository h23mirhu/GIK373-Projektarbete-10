Goal 15

Sustainable Development Goal 15 (SDG 15) aim to "protect, restore and promote sustainable use of ecosystems on land, sustainably manage forests, combat desertification, halt and reverse land degradation and halt biodiversity loss" (Globala MÃ¥len, 2024). Forests are central to the achievement of the SDG 15, as they are not only vital for biodiversity but also act as carbon sinks, protect water cycles and stabilize soils. Among the targets within this goal, Indicator 15.1.1 (forest area as a proportion of total land area) stands out as a measurable and reliable metric for assessing progress. In our work, we focus on this specific indicator, supported by data visualisations showing how forest area in Europe and Sweden has evolved between the years 2000 and 2020.
