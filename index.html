Goal 15

Sustainable Development Goal 15 (SDG 15) aim to "protect, restore and promote sustainable use of ecosystems on land, sustainably manage forests, combat desertification, halt and reverse land degradation and halt biodiversity loss" (UNDP Sverige, 2024). Forests are central to the achievement of the SDG 15, as they are not only vital for biodiversity but also act as carbon sinks, protect water cycles and stabilize soils. In our work, we focus on this specific goal, supported by data visualisations showing how forests in Europe and Sweden have evolved between the years 2000 and 2020.

Europe

Within Europe, Goal 15 plays a crucial role in shaping environmental policies focused on ecological recovery and long-term sustainability. Given the continent's diverse climates and history of land use, the sustainable managements of forests is both a priority and a challenge.
